About

For the past two years, I’ve been studying interaction design at Malmö University. I was briefly employed by Arvato, putting together prototypes for their online payment solution. Before that, I helped maintain city parks, worked as a translator, farmed cucumbers, studied art history, and got a master’s degree in graphic design.

In practice, my erratic background and hybrid skills make me do well at the meeting point of interaction aesthetics and technology. Most often, this means asking questions through prototypes. Depending on the situation, I sketch with pen and paper, hardware, software, electronics, or code.

That said, I am concerned with the why (or why not) of design, and believe design to be at its most meaningful when rooted in people’s everyday lives. What drives me, really, is a curiosity about how people and things shape each other.
